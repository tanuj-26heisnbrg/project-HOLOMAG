DISASTERS

Disasters are events or occurrences that cause significant disruption, damage, destruction, and loss to life, property, and the environment. These events can be natural or human-made and often have widespread impacts on communities and societies. Here are some common types of disasters:

Disasters Information

Natural Disasters Definition
Sudden shaking or movement of the Earth's surface caused by the release of energy from tectonic plates.
Powerful tropical storms characterized by strong winds, heavy rain, and storm surges, which can cause flooding, landslides, and widespread damage.
Overflow of water onto land that is usually dry, often caused by heavy rainfall, melting snow, or storm surges.
A tsunami is a series of large ocean waves caused by sudden disturbances such as underwater earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, landslides, or meteorite impacts.
Prolonged periods of abnormally low rainfall, leading to water shortages, crop failures, and ecosystem degradation.
Man-Made Disasters Definition
Chemical Accidents Chemical spills, explosions, or releases of hazardous materials from industrial facilities, causing environmental contamination and health risks.
Biological Accidents Biological disasters, also known as biohazards or bio-disasters, are events or emergencies caused by biological agents that pose a threat to human health, ecosystems, or the environment.
Nuclear Accidents Accidents or incidents involving the release of radioactive materials from nuclear power plants, reactors, or weapons facilities, with potential long-term health and environmental consequences.
War Armed conflicts, wars, and civil unrest can result in widespread violence, displacement, destruction of infrastructure, and humanitarian crises.
